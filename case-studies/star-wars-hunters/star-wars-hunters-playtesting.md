# Star Wars: Hunters — Playtesting and Player Advocacy

I joined the Star Wars: Hunters team in February 2022. The game had already soft-launched in a few countries. I was brought in to help with automation, but shifted fairly quickly to being an advocate for player experience, specifically performance on mobile and Nintendo Switch.

Part of my role was running the playtest process. Before each session, I selected the build with no P0 bugs and minimal P1 bugs. The whole team participated: developers, designers, and QA. We played together over Zoom, and I took notes on everything that came up. After each session, I wrote up the feedback as P3, P4, and P5 bugs and sent a summary email so nothing got lost.

One thing I started doing on my own was pulling feedback from players on the game's Discord server. Nobody asked me to. I just thought if players were already telling us what was wrong, we should be listening. I folded what I found there into the same process — logged, prioritized, passed along.

Two things I kept pushing on. The first was the main menu. It was cluttered and overwhelming — too much surface area, no clear hierarchy. I flagged it repeatedly. Before the worldwide launch, it got reworked into sub-menus. The important constraint was that the number of clicks to purchase wouldn't go up, and it didn't. The screen got cleaner without making the path to buy longer.

The second was performance. Network issues and device overheating were occurring during playtest and personal play sessions on the devices we were testing. I kept raising them. They were partially addressed before launch, not fully, but they were on the radar because someone kept putting them there.
 
The game launched worldwide. The work wasn't always a straight line from feedback to fix, but that's how it goes. The point was to make sure the player's perspective was in the room and stayed there.
